Fun & Curious Papers

Five pieces of writing I enjoyed making — some decode brains, some decode markets, some just quietly wonder what it means to live next to a machine that can almost do everything.
Xupeng Chen · author's shelf Five entries · click to read

The papers on this shelf are not reading lists, they are stories. I wrote them in between trains, in small cafés, and one of them on the floor of a hospital waiting room. If you're curious enough to click one, you already deserve the punchline.

Three of them are about AI pushing on the world — markets, inequality, macroeconomics. One is about AI reading medical images. And one is about decoding the human voice directly from the cortex. They share only this: each tries to find a small, honest handle on something very large.

A neural speech decoding framework, or: how to listen to a brain talk
